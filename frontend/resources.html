Eye Conditions Guide

Learn about the conditions detected by our AI screening system.

Cataract

Clouding of the eye's natural lens, leading to decreased vision. Common in older adults but treatable with surgery.

Common Treatable
  • Symptoms: blurred vision, glare sensitivity, faded colors
  • Treatment: phacoemulsification (surgical lens replacement)
  • Prevention: UV protection, healthy diet, regular eye exams

Conjunctivitis

Inflammation of the conjunctiva causing redness, discharge, and irritation. Can be viral, bacterial, or allergic.

Common Contagious
  • Symptoms: red eyes, watery discharge, itching
  • Treatment: depends on type — antibiotics, antivirals, or antihistamines
  • Prevention: hand hygiene, avoid touching eyes, don't share towels

Pterygium

A growth of fleshy tissue on the conjunctiva that can extend onto the cornea. Often caused by UV exposure.

Moderate Treatable
  • Symptoms: redness, irritation, potential vision obstruction
  • Treatment: artificial tears for mild cases, surgery if advanced
  • Prevention: wear UV-blocking sunglasses, use a hat outdoors

Uveitis

Inflammation of the uvea (middle layer of the eye). Can cause pain, redness, and vision loss if untreated.

Urgent Treatable
  • Symptoms: eye pain, redness, blurred vision, light sensitivity
  • Treatment: corticosteroid drops, cycloplegics, immunosuppressants
  • Note: often linked to autoimmune conditions — systemic workup recommended

Eyelid Conditions

Includes blepharitis, chalazion, and other lid margin diseases affecting comfort and vision.

Common Manageable
  • Symptoms: swelling, crusting, redness along the lid margin
  • Treatment: warm compresses, lid hygiene, topical antibiotics
  • Prevention: regular lid cleaning, avoid rubbing eyes

Healthy Eyes

Tips for maintaining good eye health and preventing common eye conditions.

Prevention
  • Get comprehensive eye exams every 1-2 years
  • Eat foods rich in omega-3, lutein, and vitamins C & E
  • Follow the 20-20-20 rule to reduce digital eye strain
  • Always wear UV-blocking sunglasses outdoors

Quick Tips for Eye Care

20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce digital eye strain.

UV Protection

Wear sunglasses that block 99-100% of UV-A and UV-B rays to prevent pterygium and cataracts.

Eye-Healthy Diet

Include leafy greens, fish rich in omega-3, eggs, nuts, and citrus fruits in your diet.

Hygiene First

Wash hands before touching eyes. Never share eye drops, makeup, or contact lenses.
